## 3. What to include in a DMCA takedown notice

To be effective under the DMCA, your written notice must include:

1. **Identification of the copyrighted work** you claim has been infringed, or a representative list if multiple works.
2. **Identification of the infringing material** on FlushPin, with enough detail to locate it (URL, business name, listing ID, screenshots).
3. **Your contact information** — name, address, telephone, and email.
4. A statement that you **have a good faith belief** that use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
5. A statement that the information in the notice is **accurate**, and under penalty of perjury, that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
6. Your **physical or electronic signature**.

Send notices to: **dmca@flushpin.com** (preferred) or the postal address above.

## 4. Our response

Upon receipt of a valid DMCA notice, FlushPin may:

- Remove or disable access to the identified material
- Notify the user who submitted the material
- Preserve records as required by law
- Terminate repeat infringers in appropriate circumstances

We aim to acknowledge valid notices within **2 business days** and act within **5 business days**, but timing is not guaranteed.

---

## 5. Counter-notification

If you believe material was removed by mistake or misidentification, you may send a **counter-notification** to dmca@flushpin.com including:

1. Identification of the removed material and its prior location
2. Your name, address, phone, and email
3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the material was removed due to mistake or misidentification
4. Consent to jurisdiction of the **Federal District Court for the Central District of California** (or your local district if outside the U.S.)
5. Consent to accept service of process from the original complainant
6. Your physical or electronic signature

We may restore material after **10–14 business days** unless the complainant files a court action, subject to applicable law.

## 6. Repeat infringer policy

FlushPin may suspend or terminate accounts of users who are **repeat infringers** of copyright or other intellectual property rights, as determined in our sole discretion consistent with applicable law.

## 7. Misrepresentations

Under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f), any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing, or that material was removed by mistake, may be liable for damages.
